# Job Market Intelligence Dashboard

A personal job market analytics platform for students and junior job seekers.

The goal is not simply to rewrite CVs with AI, but to help users understand the job market, identify skill gaps, and make more informed career decisions through data-driven insights.

## Problem

Many job seekers save dozens of job descriptions during their job search.

However, it is difficult to answer questions such as:

* What types of roles appear most frequently?
* Which skills are currently in demand?
* What are my biggest skill gaps?
* Should I focus on QA, Full Stack, Data, or another path?
* How is the market changing over time?

Existing AI tools can analyse a single job description, but they rarely help users analyse their entire job search journey.

## Core Features

### Job Collector

Store and organise job descriptions.

* Company
* Title
* Location
* Link
* Job Description
* Application Status
* Date Saved

### Role Classifier

Automatically classify jobs into categories such as:

* QA / Testing
* Full Stack
* Backend
* Data
* Helpdesk
* Graduate Programs

### Market Analytics

Analyse saved job descriptions to identify:

* Most requested skills
* Most requested technologies
* Most common soft skills
* Demand trends over time

### Skill Gap Analysis

Compare market demand against a user's profile and identify:

* Missing high-demand skills
* Frequently requested certifications
* Recommended next learning priorities

### Career Path Comparison

Compare different career directions based on collected job data.

Examples:

* QA vs Full Stack
* Backend vs Data
* Graduate Programs vs Junior Roles

### Visual Dashboard

Trend Detection

Display job market trends using:

* Tables
* Bar charts
* Pie charts
* Trend graphs

Examples:
In past 3 months, Docker 20%; Recent month, Docker 58%
Therefore, Docker demand is increasing rapidly.

## Bonus Features

### CV Tailor Assistant

Generate tailored CV suggestions based on selected job descriptions.

The purpose is to support job seekers with customisation while keeping market analysis as the primary focus.
